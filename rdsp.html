A registered disability savings plan (RDSP) is a trust arrangement that is registered with the Canada Revenue Agency. Its main purpose is to give financial security to an RDSP beneficiary after they turn 60 years of age.


An eligible person or organization can:

  • open an RDSP for an eligible beneficiary’s benefit with a participating financial institution
  • contribute up to $200,000 to the RDSP until the end of the year the beneficiary turns 59 years of age
  • apply, on behalf of the beneficiary, for Government of Canada disability savings grant and bond incentives. For details: Canada disability savings grants and bonds

All contributions, rollovers, grants, bonds, and investment earnings grow tax-free while they are in an RDSP. Grants, bonds, rollovers, and investment earnings are included in the beneficiary’s income for tax purposes when withdrawn.

source

RDSP is tax-sheltered which means you do not pay tax on the money in the plan until it is withdrawn.

source
  • Anyone can contribute to plan (with permission of plan holder)
  • There is NO tax deduction associated with contribution to RDSP
  • No annual contribution limit
  • Lifetime contribution limit - $200,000
  • What money can be transferred in (i.e. rollover)
    • Situations
      • Death of caregiver parent or grandparent
      • Transferring from a Registered Education Savings Plan (RESP)
    • Rollover Maximum = Lifetime limit - contributions to RDSP
    • Governement will NOT pay matching grant money on money transferred in
source

Grant

  • Grant amount based on contribution amount and family income
  • Family Income thresholds are indexed annually by CRA (for 2021)
    • Family Income less than or equal to $106,717 (for 2021)
      • First $500
        • government will contribute $3 for every dollar
      • Amount between $500 and $1500
        • government will contribute $3 for every dollar
      • Amount > $1500
        • no government contribution - hit yearly max of $3,500
    • Family Income greater than $106,717 (for 2021)
      • government will contribute $1 for every dollar up to $1,000 each year
  • paid until Dec 31 of year you turn 49
  • yearly grant limit is $3,500
  • lifetime grant limit is $70,000

Bond

  • Whether receive bond based on Family income
  • No contribution required - automatically received if eligible
  • bond available for low and modest income individuals only
  • yearly bond limit is $1,000
  • lifetime bond limt is $20,000

Family Income

  • Beneficiaries age 18 and under
    • Family income is combined income of their parents and guardians
    • Beneficary must start filling personal income tax returns every beginning in the year they turn 17 so they recieve the correct amounts of grants/bonds in the year they turn 19
  • Beneficiaries age 19 and over
    • Based on own income plus their spouse's income
    • Beneficary must start filling personal income tax returns every beginning in the year they turn 17 so they recieve the correct amounts of grants/bonds in the year they turn 19
    • Must sign grant and bond applicaiton with financial institution to provide their consent for the government to retireve their income information

Carry-forward

  • Can receive more grants/bonds in a year if you were approved for Disability Tax Credit for any or all of the past 10 years

source
  • No restrictions on withdrawals
  • RDSP can be invested on a tax deferred basis
  • Can be opened up until December 31 of the year a beneficiary turns 59
  • If the beneficiary is 49 or younger
    • contributions to the account receive additional government grants
    • if their income is low, they may also receive government bonds
    • grants and bonds can be much higher than the contributions themselves, so this may be very lucrative
  • you can leave your RRSP or RRIF to a financially dependent child or grandchild upon your death
  • inheritance (some or all) can be deposited to their RDSP on a tax-deferred basis
  • $200,000 lifetime limit for an RDSP account: includes contributions as well as the allowable tax-deferred transfer from an RRSP or RRIF account
  • RDSP generally do not impact government benefits that are based on income thresholds
  • RRSP/RRIF can be transferred to RDSP to defer tax. Does this need to be specified in will?
  • Open RDSP when you are approved for Disability Tax Credit
    • can only carry forward grants/bonds from past 10 years
    • if disability started prior to 10 years ago, can not bet grants/bond for year older than 10 years
  • Start submitting tax return once agee 17
    • Beneficary must start filling personal income tax returns every beginning in the year they turn 17 so they will recieve the correct amounts of grants/bonds in the year they turn 19 based upon their own income
  • To take money before 59 - set date for 10 year marker
    • If you take out money from RDSP, grant/bonds in last 10 years need to be repaid
    • You can request them to stop grant/bonds to RDSP which sets date for 10 year marker
    • 10 years after marker date (even if not 59), you take out money
