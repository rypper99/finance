RRIF : Registered Retirement Income Fund

  • RRIF is for asset decumulation where assets grow tax free
  • Types
    • personal RRIFS
    • spousal RRIFS - spousal RRSP converted to RRIF
  • When
  • RRIF can be created at any age before 71
  • RRSP must be converted to RRIF by age 71
  • Withdrawals
    • RRIF must generate income
    • Withdrawals can be monthly, quarterly, sem-annually or annually
    • RRIF has minimum payment amount (as per table based on age) but no maximum
    • Minimum withdrawal calculated by multiplying market value on Jan 1 by prescrived withdrawal factor
    • Withdrawal factor based on age - can be based on spouse's age
    • RRIF Withdrawals can be scheduled payments and/or lump sum payments
    • No withdrawal necessary in year RRIF was setup
    • Withdrawals can be made in kind
    • If you have multiple RRIFs, must withdraw at least minimum annual amount from EACH account
  • Taxes
    • All withdrawals are fully taxable
    • Minimum RRIF payments - no withholding taxes deducted
    • Greater than minimum RRIF payment s- Withholding taxes required on withdraw
    • When 65, can split up to 50% of eligible pension income with spouse
    • When 65, income from RRIF qualifies for up to $2,000 towards the Pension Income Credit
  • Beneficiary
    • If spouse is beneficiary - it can be transferred tax-free to spouse's RRIF or RRSP
    • If spouse is successor annuitant - spouse can take over RRIF tax free and start receiving RRIF payments
    • Beneficiary - Leave RRIF to child or estate
  • Open at 65 you get pension tax credit
  • Open before 71 to leverage lower tax rates
  • Don't have to change investment strategy in RRIF
    • Will need to consider how it will generate income, Plan what comes out and when
  • Time Line Investing
    • Cash (years 1-3)
    • Income / Balanced (years 4-6)
    • Growth (years 7-10)
      • High quality / diversification with high quality blue chip stocks and ETFs
      • Periodically harvest profits
  • RRIF must generate income
    • If don't need income, can do in-kind transfer to non-reg account
    • You will need to have cash oriented assets ready to pay out when you need income
  • Annual RRIF withdrawal near end of year - money grows inside RRIF during year
