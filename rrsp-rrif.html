  • RRSP vs TFSA
    • Assumptions:
      • Earn $5,000 in top bracket (50% tax) - pay $2,500 in tax
      • RRSP and TFSA invested the same
      • RRSP and TFSA balances will triple over time period
    • RRSP
      • contribute $5,000 to RRSP
        • $2,500 from after tax money
        • $2,500 from tax return - due to $5,000 RRSP contribution
      • initial value of $5,000
      • triples to $15,000
      • withdraw (assume 50% tax rate) = $7,500
    • TFSA
      • contribute $2,500 to TFSA
        • no extra to contribute as no tax deducation for TFSA
      • initial value of $2,000
      • triples to $7,500
      • withdraw (not taxed) = $7,500
    • Summary
      • assuming 50% tax rate - both RRSP and TFSA will yield $7,500
      • what if your tax rate is lower at withdrawal (i.e. 30%)
        • RRSP returns $10,500 (only 30% tax on withdraw)
        • TFSA returns $7,500 (no benfit if withdrawing in lower tax bracket)
  • Open at 65 you get pension tax credit
  • Open before 71 to leverage lower tax rates
  • Don't have to change investment strategy in RRIF
    • Will need to consider how it will generate income, Plan what comes out and when
  • Time Line Investing
    • Cash (years 1-3)
    • Income / Balanced (years 4-6)
    • Growth (years 7-10)
      • High quality / diversification with high quality blue chip stocks and ETFs
      • Periodically harvest profits
  • RRIF must generate income
    • If don't need income, can do in-kind transfer to non-reg account
    • You will need to have cash oriented assets ready to pay out when you need income
  • Annual RRIF withdrawal near end of year - money grows inside RRIF during year
