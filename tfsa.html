TFSA: Tax-Free Savings Account

  • On Death
    • beneficiary
      • can be spouse (who is not successor holder), former spouses, common-law partners, children, etc.
      • if spouse named as beneficary, they have until Dec 31 of the year following the year of death to contribute any payments received out of TFSA, up to the date of death value into their own TFSA without affecting their own unused contribution room.
        • To complete this "exempt contribution", they must file CRA Form RC240 with 30 days after the contribution is made.
    • successor holder
      • can only be surviving spouse or common-law partner
      • TFSA continues to exist and the successor holder assumes ownership of the TFSA contract and all of its contents
      • Successor Holder can directly transfer part or all of the value to their own TFSA and not affect available TFSA contribution room
